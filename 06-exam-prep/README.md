## Overview

This is the consolidation section. By now you've worked through Foundations, Data Preparation,
Application Development, Assembling and Deploying, and Governance/Evaluation/Monitoring. This section
does not teach new material — it helps you **verify coverage against the official blueprint**, **find
and close your own gaps**, **rehearse under timed conditions**, and **walk in with a checklist of the
mistakes that most commonly cost points**.

## Learning Outcomes

By completing this section, you will be able to:

- Map every official exam objective to the repo content that covers it and spot your weak areas
- Diagnose your own knowledge gaps with a structured self-assessment and remediation plan

- Apply a disciplined process for multiple-choice and multi-select questions under pressure
- Recognize and avoid the specific traps and stale-terminology distractors this exam uses
- Run a final pre-exam checklist covering both knowledge and logistics

## How This Section Fits

Sections 01–05 built the knowledge; this section turns it into exam readiness. It is deliberately
last and deliberately synthetic — it references the earlier sections rather than introducing anything
new, so use it as a pointer back into the material wherever a gap surfaces.

## Study Tips

- **Do the gap analysis first, honestly.** The point isn't to feel prepared — it's to find the
  objectives you're weakest on while there's still time to fix them.
- **Simulate the real thing at least once:** 60 questions, 90 minutes, no notes. Timed practice
  reveals pacing problems that untimed review hides.
- **Study the pitfalls chapter like flashcards.** Many exam points are lost not to ignorance but to
  stale terminology and near-miss distractors — knowing the traps is worth several questions.
